About Me

My path from the lab bench to the data pipeline.

I started my career as a biomedical scientist working in clinical diagnostics — processing patient samples, running antimicrobial susceptibility tests, and generating lab reports at a diagnostic facility in Egypt. That's where I first realized that the data coming out of the lab was just as important as the benchwork itself.

I wanted to do more with that data. I pursued a diploma in bioinformatics and computational biology at the German University in Cairo, trained in deep learning for biomedical applications at Nile University, and completed Stanford's precision medicine data science program. I earned the Google Advanced Data Analytics Professional Certificate and built hands-on projects in healthcare ML, clinical data analysis, and business intelligence.

Today I'm based in Riyadh, Saudi Arabia, looking for opportunities where I can combine my clinical domain expertise with my data skills — analyzing healthcare data, building dashboards, supporting clinical research, or working on health AI initiatives. I'm especially drawn to roles that sit at the intersection of healthcare and data, where understanding what the data actually means matters as much as knowing how to query it.
